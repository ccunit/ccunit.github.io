Bandung City

Introduction
Bandung is the capital city of West Java province of Indonesia. Located in the island of Java, Greater Bandung is the country's second-largest and most populous metropolitan area, with over 11 million inhabitants.

Bandung, in the past, was a city of rich history, culture, and natural beauty nestled in the highlands of West Java, Indonesia. Its transformation from a small village to a bustling metropolis is a testament to the city's dynamic past.

During the early 20th century, Bandung underwent a dramatic transformation as part of the Dutch East Indies' modernization efforts. It was envisioned as a model city, with wide tree-lined avenues, parks, and elegant Art Deco and Neo-Classical buildings. This period saw the construction of iconic landmarks like the Gedung Sate, a government building with a unique architectural design.


What was once a peaceful city, now turned into a large urban metropolitan area indundated with traffic jams.
